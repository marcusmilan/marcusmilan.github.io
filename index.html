About me

I am a sociobiologist at Yale, where I do research on genetics of social networks and the evolution of human cooperation. I was born in the Balkans and, since turning 16, I have lived and worked in 6 countries and 10 American cities. I did my undergradaute work at Yale, my graduate work at Oxford and Harvard, and my postdoctoral research at Stanford. After graduating, I worked for a number of California-based biotech and tech startup companies. I now live and work in New Haven, Connecticut.

I write short stories, novels and poems. Much of my writting is about identity conflict, exploring the legacy of war crimes in late 20th century Europe and the rise of neofascism in contemporary America. I am also interested in urban culture and counterculture in bicostal America, especially in San Francisco and New York. My nonfiction columns and essays explore topics in international relations, politics, public health, and science and technology.

My writings

My first novel Tenderloin is set in San Franciso at the height of the 2010's tech boom. It follows a year in a life of Aaron Green, who one New Year's Eve leaves his ordinary life and enters the underground world of durgs, prostitution and crime in San Francisco. I am currently working on my second novel, The Ghost in the Sunet. The Ghost in the Sunset is a fantasy novel about a teenage boy's fight against a clandestine religious order in the Sunset District of San Francisco.

I have also written two short stories, "Home," and "We Built Castles in the Sky." My poems include "Tombstones of a Nation," "Naked," "I just remembered somethign...", "What happened?, "Elements/Time," "life<-ifelse(...)," and others.
